# Electricity Campus – HEDGE-IoT Dutch Pilot

This repository contains relevant public information for the HEDGE-IoT Dutch pilot at Electricity Campus Arnhems Buiten.

The Dutch pilot focuses on making distributed energy assets measurable, interoperable and controllable through open standards. The goal is to validate how heterogeneous energy assets can be connected through a semantic interoperability layer and made available for monitoring, flexibility, anomaly detection and future market interaction.

Electricity Campus Arnhems Buiten acts as a living lab for grid-scale innovation. The campus has its own local electricity infrastructure and is located in a congested grid area, making it a relevant environment to test how hidden grid capacity can be unlocked through better data, flexible operation and interoperable control.

## What the pilot explores

The pilot investigates how energy assets such as buildings, PV systems, EV charging infrastructure, V2G, batteries, heat pumps and smart meters can be connected in a reusable and vendor-neutral way.

Key themes include:

- Semantic interoperability using SAREF and the HEDGE-IoT interoperability approach
- Monitoring and data exchange for distributed energy resources
- Making flexibility measurable and usable without breaching local grid constraints
- Connecting local assets to future flexibility and congestion-management services
- Supporting anomaly detection and predictive maintenance based on operational data
- Creating reusable building blocks for other campuses, business parks and energy hubs

## Why this matters

Dutch grid congestion is often presented as a purely physical capacity problem. In practice, part of the challenge is also operational and administrative: reserved grid capacity is not always used at the same time, while businesses still face limits when electrifying their operations.

HEDGE-IoT helps explore how this unused flexibility can become visible and actionable. By making assets semantically understandable and securely accessible, flexibility can be quantified, coordinated and eventually linked to market or grid signals.

## Role of Electricity Campus

Electricity Campus Arnhems Buiten provides a real-world test environment where technology providers, researchers, grid actors and energy companies can validate solutions under practical conditions.

The campus supports:

- Real energy assets and operational buildings
- Local grid constraints and congestion challenges
- A growing innovation ecosystem around electrification and digital energy systems
- Collaboration with partners such as TNO, VU Amsterdam, CGI, ABB, Oost NL and the wider HEDGE-IoT consortium

## Repository purpose

This repository provides public information about the Dutch HEDGE-IoT pilot at Electricity Campus Arnhems Buiten. It explains the pilot context, the main use cases being explored, and selected components developed or demonstrated as part of the project.

The repository can be used as a starting point for partners, researchers and other interested parties who want to understand what has been built, how the pilot is structured, and where to find more detailed information about specific components.
